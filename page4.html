What can we do?

Change....

We need to start helping our oceans. We can do this by recycling, cutting emissions, moving to green energy and ensuring waste does not enter the oceans. We should reduce our plastic use. Plastic is a very useful human invention it can be used for containers, medical equipment, car parts and so much more. Yet when it is dumped it washes into the rivers due to its buoyancy and floats out to sea where it is eaten by fish and other animals causing severe issues. This is of course accidental on the fishes behalf but to us its not, we have a choice. We can reuse the plastic or better, stop producing it altogether. The production of plastic causes massive greenhouse pollution which causes acid rain to fall into our oceans. We can use other less harmful materials such as bamboo, wood, natural fibre and also bioplastics. These are all sustainable.


Start cleaning our beaches, removing harmful debris and materials that have washed up and disposing of them in the correct manner. This would be a great for school children to do on summer holidays as part of an awareness campaign. We need to get serious about recycling. In Ireland, there's a scheme called Return, where a deposit of 15 or 25 cent is added to the price of drinks in cans or bottles. This extra charge encourages people to return the empty containers to local machines, where they can get their deposit back. This is a great initiative and it works! We need to hold countries responsible for their actions. This is extremely important. The worlds biggest economies are the biggest polluters. This needs to change.
